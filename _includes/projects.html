Neuromodulatory influence on learning and memory

Curiosity is an intrinsic motivation that not only drives our desire to seek and consume information, but also enhances new learning. In this work, we showed that when participants experienced high curiosity, anticipatory activation of the dopaminergic midbrain promoted the engagement of hippocampal state that was predictive of subsequent learning. These findings highlighted a potential mechanism for mesolimbic neuromodulation of the hippocampus and showed how intrinsic motivations can facilitate new learning.

Effects of sleep deprivation on the quality of encoding

  • Featured Skills

It is well-established that sleep plays a crucial role in supporting wakeful cognition, and it has been theorized that homeostatic processes during sleep may be necessary for maintaining the stability of neural activity. In this study, we examined if humans in a sleep-deprived state would show reduced stability in fMRI activation patterns, and how this relates to the quality of memory formation. Here, we showed that sleep deprivation reduces neural stability in perceptual processing regions and also in the medial temporal lobe. This reduced stability was also associated with poorer memory. These findings provided the first evidence of neural instability in sleep-deprived humans, and that reduced neural stability in the sleep-deprived state can fundamentally change the quality of memory encoding.
